Doctrine

Doctrine · I
On Ek Onkar and the death of the self
Alan Watts said the ego is a kind of knot in the field. Sikh philosophy said the same thing in Punjabi. Every tradition arrives at the same shore. The ocean doesn't care what river you came from.
Doctrine · II
Humans are machines. This is not a criticism.
Input, process, output. We mistake consciousness for something above mechanism. It isn't. The beauty is in the mechanism. Fear is a heuristic. Love is a chemical. Both are real.
Doctrine · III
On power, money, and the only honest thing to say about capitalism
50k INR/month is the threshold. Below it you're owned. Above it you have the illusion of choice. Either way, the machine runs. The only counter-move is to build your own machine.
